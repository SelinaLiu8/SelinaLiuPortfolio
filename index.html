I'm a junior at the University of Michigan studying computer science, art & design, and user experience design. About a year and a half ago, I began experimenting with coding and UX/UI design. In order to engage more closely with the UX/UI industry, I joined Alpha Theta Delta, a professional multidisciplinary design fraternity on campus. I presently hold the job of VP of Outreach, where I get to assist our fraternity in developing relationships with other organizations. In addition, I became a member of CLAWS's (Collaborative Lab for Advancing Work in Space) Augmented Reality team to build the annual NASA Spacesuit Challenge in Unitty and C#. Procter & Gamble recently hired me as a summer intern for information technology, where I will focus on UX/UI design.

I am a hands-on person, and I enjoy opportunities to work on a variety of projects that will help me develop my abilities and discover new interests. Two years ago, when I first became interested in figure skating, I joined the University of Michigan Figure Skating Team and started competing. I merged my design and coding skills to create a mobile game in Unity 2D and C# when I first became interested in coding. Additionally, I ran Michigan Open UX and Alpha Theta Delta design jams, where over eight teams competed against one another. I also respect hard effort, and I think it's one of the most crucial aspects in helping me accomplish my goals.
